\begin{abstract}
We describe the teleoperation stack of a dual-arm SO-101 (LeRobot) rig for
garment manipulation, driven by Meta Quest hand controllers. The stack
combines a clutched absolute-pose retargeting scheme with a
reachable-by-construction orientation mapping (``armplane'') tailored to the
5-DoF kinematics of the SO-101, One-Euro input filtering, and a pluggable
inverse-kinematics layer with six interchangeable methods (two Pink QP
variants, damped least squares, mink, bounded nonlinear least squares, and a
Telegrip-style split IK with a direct wrist mapping). We introduce an
analytic workspace envelope with four selectable out-of-envelope target
policies (warn, project, freeze, slowdown) and evaluate all components in a
MuJoCo digital twin: a tracking suite, a new wrist-agility suite that
quantifies orientation lag, and a new out-of-envelope suite that measures
behavior while the operator's hand exceeds the arm's reach. The Telegrip
split IK reduces roll tracking lag from \SI{300}{ms} to \SI{120}{ms} at
\SI{1}{Hz} wrist oscillation relative to the production QP tracker, at the
cost of higher command jerk, and the projection policy keeps boundary
tracking error bounded (\(\approx\)\SI{26}{mm} versus \SI{131}{mm} for the
legacy no-op behavior) with seamless re-entry. Implementation details of all
methods are given in the appendix.
\end{abstract}
