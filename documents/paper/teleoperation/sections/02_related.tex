\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{VR teleoperation of low-cost arms.}
Telegrip~\citep{telegrip} teleoperates SO-100 arms from WebXR controllers
with a deliberately minimal scheme: PyBullet position-only IK on the three
proximal joints and a direct mapping from controller rotation deltas to the
two wrist joints, with joint limits clamped after the solve. Its wrist
feels agile precisely because the wrist path bypasses any task-space
optimization. Our \texttt{telegrip} method (Section~\ref{sec:telegrip})
ports this split-IK idea to our benchmark interface, replacing PyBullet
with the simulator's own kinematics and recovering the wrist angles
analytically from the absolute target orientation. Other VR pipelines for
hobby-class arms (e.g.\ Dream-Machines' vr-teleop-kit, whose damped
least-squares strategy our \texttt{dls} baseline reproduces) typically
track the full 6D pose and inherit the over-constraint problem on 5-DoF
hardware.

\paragraph{Differential IK and task-space control.}
Our production solver builds on Pink~\citep{pink}, a task-based
differential-IK library over Pinocchio~\citep{pinocchio}, solving a QP with
frame-tracking, damping, and posture tasks under joint limits;
mink~\citep{mink} solves the analogous problem over MuJoCo models. Damped
least squares is the classical singularity-robust baseline
\citep{wampler1986,nakamura1986}. The benchmark also includes a per-tick
bounded nonlinear least-squares solve (\texttt{scipy\_ls}) as an
optimization-based reference.

\paragraph{Input filtering.}
Controller pose noise is attenuated with the One-Euro
filter~\citep{oneeuro}, the de-facto standard for interactive input: a
first-order low-pass whose cutoff adapts to signal speed, trading jitter at
rest against lag during fast motion. Filter parameters interact directly
with wrist agility (Section~\ref{sec:experiments}).

\paragraph{Workspace awareness.}
Commercial teleoperation stacks commonly project infeasible targets onto a
precomputed reachability approximation; we adopt the same philosophy with
an analytic annulus-plus-floor envelope derived from the URDF
(Section~\ref{sec:envelope}), and additionally compare \emph{freeze} and
\emph{velocity-shaping} alternatives, which to our knowledge have not been
compared head-to-head on low-cost 5-DoF hardware.
